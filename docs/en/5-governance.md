# 5 · Authorship, data and resources

The network's rules. Accepted when a project activates and agreed before any submission.

## Authorship and credit

- **The young researcher is first author by design.** The network exists to advance their career. First place is theirs unless the team agrees and records otherwise.
- **CRediT taxonomy.** Each output carries a statement of who did what, kept in the project repo.
- **A mentor is senior author only with genuine intellectual contribution.** Accompanying is not co-authorship.
- **Infrastructure is not authorship.** GitHub, compute, Overleaf, coordination and this support go in acknowledgements, never as an author. The network does not seek billing; with a low profile, it does not even ask to appear except in a brief acknowledgement.
- **Data and code** are published with a DOI (Zenodo) crediting the same person as first responsible, unless ethics or privacy restricts it.

Suggested acknowledgement, brief and discreet:

> This work was supported by coordination and infrastructure from a network of Venezuelan researchers. The authors retain full responsibility for the content.

## Consent and data use

Aligned to a GDPR-style standard, since there are members in the EU.

- **What we collect:** a capability profile and a private contact. We do not collect the member's sensitive data for the directory.
- **What for:** matching ideas with people, assessing fit for calls, and drafting. Legal basis: consent. Not sold or shared outside the network.
- **AI processing:** the profile may be processed with AI to match and draft, with explicit, separate consent. Without it, the profile is kept but not processed with AI.
- **Rights:** view, correct, delete and withdraw consent at any time.
- **Sensitive research data** (clinical, mental health, case geolocation): never in public repos. The project starts private and needs a data plan and ethics clearance before moving anything. Synthetic or aggregated data preferred for public examples.

Consent record, three dated checkboxes: use of profile to coordinate (required), AI processing (optional), listing in the internal directory (optional).

## Stages and resources

Resources are released at milestones, not on sign-up. Cheap things are given generously; compute when the work needs it.

| Resource | Scoped | Active | Targeting a output | Delivered |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Template repo and board | Yes | Yes | Yes | Archived |
| Private data repo | No | If sensitive data | Yes | Archived |
| Overleaf | No | Basic | Premium for the team | Yes |
| Zotero group | No | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Compute / GPU | No | Only if demonstrably needed | As needed | Close out |
| Zenodo DOI | No | No | Yes | Yes |
| Cash prize | No | No | If justified | Close out |

A project that stalls is not deleted: it drops to a visible dormant state and can revive.

## Profile and intake

Each member's profile is stored as YAML in a private repo. The intake is dual: it collects the capability profile and emergency-response availability in one pass.

Profile fields: name, career stage, affiliation, country, whether inside Venezuela and in which state, disciplines, methods and techniques they can run, instruments and datasets they access, software and compute, languages, hours per week, what they seek, what they offer, output goals, links, consent, and an emergency-response block (available, which group, hours, and whether directly affected). Contact and email live only in the private repo.

Intake questions, in order: identity and location (with the state inside Venezuela), capabilities, what they seek and offer, links and optional CV, an emergency-response section, and at the end the three consent checkboxes with the full notice linked.

## Note

Before opening the intake and moving member or patient data, someone with a data-protection background should review the consent and the sensitive-data path, especially given members in the EU.
