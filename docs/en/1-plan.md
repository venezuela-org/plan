# 1 · The plan

Conexión RedJIV's response to the earthquakes of June 24, 2026. We take the network's talent and ideas and put them to work on useful things, now. People join a group by expertise and start without guessing why they are there.

Living document. Updated early July 2026.

## Low profile by design

The first rule. We do not seek to be famous or known. The work matters more than the name. Credit belongs to the people who do it, not to the network, which is only a discreet enabler. This is not false modesty: in an environment with more than 200 blocked websites and surveillance over communications, a low profile also protects those inside the country. We work quietly and let the results and the people speak.

## The situation

As of early July 2026, per the UN and Venezuelan authorities and subject to change: at least 2,595 dead, around 12,400 injured, nearly 50,000 unaccounted for, and more than 860 aftershocks. Two magnitude 7.2 and 7.5 earthquakes 39 seconds apart on June 24 at 6:04 pm, epicenters near Yumare (Yaracuy). La Guaira is a disaster zone; Caracas and several states severely damaged. Around 58,870 buildings damaged, losses of roughly US$6.7 billion (about 6% of GDP), and 1.8 million people in need, 680,000 of them children. The health system is at breaking point and the UN warns of infectious disease. Figures are preliminary and rising.

## How we work

Groups ready by expertise. Each group has an experienced lead who validates the work and connects with responders on the ground, and room for master's, doctoral and undergraduate students to execute and come out as first authors of the result. Different levels, one group.

Everything is channeled through already-established bodies (UN OCHA, UNICEF, Direct Relief, FUNVISIS). We do not improvise in the zone. The network's advantage is remote analytical and information work, the diaspora's reach, and verification from those inside the country.

## Time horizons

- **Now (first weeks).** Activate the groups. Needs-and-access map, water guide for camps, direct support to responders.
- **2 to 3 months.** First deliverables per group: maps, protocols, shelter surveillance, psychosocial material. Sustained support to the response.
- **1 year.** From response to recovery: open data, environmental and health monitoring, and a standing science-for-emergencies capacity that does not disband. Training along the way.
- **2 years.** Contribution to reconstruction with earthquake-resistant, water and health criteria. The capacity remains as permanent muscle, and several people with outputs in their own name.
